---
title: Custom Domains
description: Serve a service on your own domain with automatic TLS
---

Every exposed service gets a generated `*.fractal.omni.dev` domain automatically.
To serve it on your own domain instead, add a custom domain from the service's
**Settings** tab.

## Add a domain

1. Open the service and go to **Settings → Networking**.
2. Enter your domain (for example `app.example.com`) and save (or press Enter).
3. Fractal shows the **DNS records** to add at your domain host. Copy each record
   into your DNS provider.
4. The certificate is issued automatically once the records resolve. The
   **Certificate** badge advances to _Active_, with no downtime.

<Callout title="Zero-downtime issuance">
Fractal uses DNS-based certificate validation, so the certificate is issued and
ready before any traffic moves to your domain. Your existing
`*.fractal.omni.dev` URL keeps working the whole time.
</Callout>

## DNS records

You typically add two records:

| Record | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Traffic `CNAME` | Points your domain at Fractal's edge |
| Verification record | Proves domain ownership so the certificate can issue |

The certificate is validated and served for you; the exact record names, types,
and values are shown in the dashboard for your specific domain, each with a copy
button.

## apex and www

Set **www** to `redirect` in the same Networking section to send
`www.example.com` to the apex `example.com` automatically. This is the common
"apex plus www" setup and needs no second custom-domain slot.

## Certificate status

The **Certificate** badge reflects where your domain is in setup:

- **Pending** &mdash; waiting for the DNS records to resolve
- **Verifying** &mdash; confirming you own the domain
- **Issuing** &mdash; the certificate is being issued
- **Active** &mdash; the certificate is live and auto-renewing
- **Error** &mdash; something needs attention (usually a missing or incorrect DNS
  record); double-check the records shown in the dashboard

Your existing `*.fractal.omni.dev` URL keeps serving the whole time, so there is
never a gap while a custom domain finishes setting up.

## Plan limits

Custom domains add no per-domain setup on your side beyond the DNS records, so the
only limit is how many your plan includes. If a domain is gated by your plan, the
panel shows an upgrade prompt instead of DNS records, and the service keeps serving
on its generated subdomain. See
[fractal.omni.dev](https://fractal.omni.dev) for current plan limits.
